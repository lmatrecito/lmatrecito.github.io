About Me

I am an astrophysics Ph.D. candidate at RIT that specializes in supermassive black holes in merging galaxies, specifically in searching for (1) hidden Active Galactic Nuclei in Hydroxyl Megamaser Galaxies, and (2) recoiling supermassive black holes in the Sloan Digital Sky Survey. I received my B.S. in Physics and Astronomy from Northern Arizona University and my M.S. in Astrophysical Sciences and Technology from RIT.

As a first-generation Mexican American female, I have experienced the difficulties minorities face in pursuing higher education, especially in STEM. This led to a passion for promoting diversity and inclusivity in the scientific community by making a positive impact on society through research and outreach work.
